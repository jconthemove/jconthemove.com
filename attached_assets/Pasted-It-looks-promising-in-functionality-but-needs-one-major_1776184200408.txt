It looks promising in functionality, but needs one major compliance redesign:
Do not let staking itself buy better odds in a random drawing if non-stakers can’t get an equal free path. In sweepstakes/lottery analysis, the danger zone is the combination of prize + chance + consideration. FTC guidance says a sweepstakes must not require payment or purchase to enter or improve odds, and Michigan consumer guidance says a buyer/donor cannot get an advantage over a free entrant. �

That can be read as pay/lock value → receive more entries into a prize drawing. If chance determines the winner, and staking increases odds, regulators may view that as adding consideration to a chance-based prize system. 2. Participation lane

Participation = non-cash, non-random utility unlocked by staking or activity.

Those are useful because they are not random cash yield and do not directly buy better odds in a drawing.
